Azizi Urban Hydrosystems & Resilience Lab

Advancing resilient urban water, environmental, infrastructure, and community systems.

The UHR Lab studies how urban hydrosystems interact with climate risk, infrastructure, environmental change, community knowledge, and decision-making to support more resilient, equitable, and sustainable cities.

Research

Research themes

Our work connects physical water processes with infrastructure systems, environmental change, social behavior, governance, and policy decisions.

≈

Urban Flooding & Climate Risk

Flood exposure, stormwater systems, compound hazards, infrastructure performance, and uneven climate risk across communities.

◌

Water, Environment & Sustainability

Urban hydrosystems, water affordability, conservation, human-environment interactions, and sustainability-oriented planning.

⌂

GSI, Behavior & Adoption

Household green stormwater infrastructure adoption, social influence, affordability, behavioral factors, and spatial flood exposure.

◇

Governance & Community Knowledge

Citizen science, stakeholder perceptions, governance networks, cross-sector coordination, and community-informed adaptation.

⟲

Socio-Hydrology & Coupled Systems

Feedbacks among people, institutions, infrastructure, water systems, and environmental change.

▦

Data, Modeling & Decision Support

Geospatial analysis, hydrologic modeling, agent-based modeling, network analysis, remote sensing, and practical decision tools.

Projects

Current and emerging directions

Projects in the UHR Lab connect scientific analysis with applied questions in flood risk, infrastructure resilience, community adaptation, and sustainability.

01

Flood risk burden and insurance protection gaps

Examining how flood losses, insurance coverage gaps, public residual losses, and institutional burdens vary across urban communities.

Flood risk Insurance gaps Equity
02

Green stormwater infrastructure adoption

Modeling how household behavior, social influence, affordability, environmental perception, and flood exposure shape GSI adoption.

ABM Stormwater Behavior
03

Flood governance networks and stakeholder perception

Studying collaboration, coordination, and perception across organizations involved in flood risk management and adaptation planning.

Governance Networks Perception
04

Community-informed urban flood modeling

Integrating resident knowledge, citizen science, and hydrologic modeling to improve flood risk understanding and local decision-making.

Citizen science Hydrology Decision support
