Azizi Urban Hydrosystems & Resilience Lab

Advancing resilient urban water, environmental, infrastructure, and community systems.

The UHR Lab studies how urban hydrosystems interact with climate risk, infrastructure systems, environmental change, community knowledge, and decision-making to support more resilient, equitable, and sustainable cities.

Explore Research Join the Lab

Research focus

Coupled urban water systems

We connect hydrologic modeling, geospatial analysis, network science, agent-based modeling, and community-engaged research to understand climate risk and adaptation in cities.

What we study

Research at the intersection of water, environment, infrastructure, and communities

Urban Flooding & Climate Risk

Flood exposure, stormwater systems, climate hazards, infrastructure performance, and uneven risk across communities.

Water, Environment & Sustainability

Urban hydrosystems, water affordability, conservation, human-environment interactions, and sustainability-oriented planning.

Community & Decision Support

Citizen science, stakeholder perceptions, governance networks, behavioral adoption, and practical tools for climate adaptation.

Featured directions

Current and emerging lab themes

01

Flood risk burden and protection gaps

Understanding how flood losses, insurance coverage, public residual losses, and institutional burdens vary across urban communities.

02

Green stormwater infrastructure adoption

Modeling how household behavior, social influence, affordability, flood exposure, and environmental attitudes shape GSI adoption.

03

Community-informed flood governance

Studying how local knowledge, stakeholder perception, governance networks, and cross-sector coordination affect flood resilience decisions.
